Designing for an Online Course
Adapted from: Quality Matters. (2023, October). Bridge to quality: A QM online course design guide basic edition. Retrieved from: https://qualitymatters.org/higher-ed-bridge-guide-basic
Links to an external site.

When choosing and creating course content:
1	Prioritize academic integrity by clearly identifying the source material and adhering to copyright law.
2	Ensure content variety, including different types of materials beyond textbooks, reflecting current thought in the field.
3	Ensure easy accessibility of all materials, such as short videos with clear audio and closed captions, and readable text.
4	Proactively meet accessibility requirements for all content, focusing on already accessible materials and creating accessible content when necessary.
Course materials should be designed to facilitate readability and minimize distractions. This involves breaking up large blocks of text, using headings for organization, and ensuring appropriate font size, type, formatting, and color contrast. Avoiding excessive use of color or purely decorative images is recommended to prevent cognitive distractions. Additionally, text alignment and use of multimedia controls should be optimized for accessibility and ease of use.
For multimedia content, educators should prioritize good audio quality, shorter video length, and clear resolution to maintain visibility of playback controls. Interactive elements should allow learners to control motion and navigation features.
Providing accessible text-based materials is crucial for supporting the success of all online learners and ensuring compliance with accessibility policies. Educators should use the Canvas Accessibility Checker

Before we acquire a new database, the following criteria must be met:  
1. Is the database based on IP authentication or SAML authentication?  
2. If it is a full-text database, does the vendor provide records in Alma Community Zone? If not, do they provide MARC records for discovery?  
3. Does the database provide 508 Compliance access?  
4.  Does the vendor have a VPAT?  
5. Does the resource provide statistics, such as COUNTER statistics?  
6. Will the vendor accept payment by purchase order (PO) and email the library an invoice? 
7. If it is a new vendor, is the purchase just a single item?    
The following criteria are optional, but it would be ideal to have them: 
1. Can the resource use OCLC EZproxy?  
2. Does the database support an OpenURL link resolver, specifically the Alma link resolver?  
3. Does the resource have an administrative interface?  

If the resource is streaming media, the following questions will need to be clarified as well.
1. Are the films captioned or do they have subtitles?
2. What format will the files be provided?  (For DSL)
3. Does the vendor provide MARC records for the films?
